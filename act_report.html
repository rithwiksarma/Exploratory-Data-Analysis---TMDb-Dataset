Introduction¶

The dataset that I wrangling (and analyzed and visualized) is the tweet archive of Twitter user @dog_rates, also known as WeRateDogs. WeRateDogs is a Twitter account that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. The ratings almost always have a denominator of 10. However, the numerators, are always greater than 10. 11/10, 12/10, 13/10, etc. This is because WeRateDogs believes that dogs deserve a much higher rating than 10 because they're 'good dogs'. WeRateDogs has over 4 million followers and has received international media coverage.

The WeRateDogs Twitter archive contains basic tweet data for all 5000+ of their tweets, but not everything. Hence the data was prepared by gathering from three different sources, assessing it in python and then cleaning the same

Twitter is a free social networking microblogging service that allows registered members to broadcast short posts called tweets. Twitter members can broadcast tweets and follow other users' tweets by using multiple platforms and devices. Tweets and replies to tweets can be sent by cell phone text message, desktop client or by posting at the Twitter.com website.

Visualization 2: Which dog stage gets most tweets?¶

There are different dog stages in a life:

Doggo

  • A doggo is a big pupper, usually older. Although, this label does not stop a doggo from behaving like a pupper.
  • A pupper that appears to have its life in order. Probably understands taxes and what not

Pupper

  • A small doggo but, usually younger. Can be equally if not more mature than some doggos.
  • A doggo that is inexperienced and unprepared for any responsibilites associated with a doggo.

Puppo

  • A transitional phase between a pupper and a doggo. Can be understood as a dog equivalent of a teenager.
  • A dog with both pupper and doggo tendencies.

Floof

  • A label given to dogs with seemingly excess fur. Comical amounts of fur on a dog will certainly earn this dog a generic name.
